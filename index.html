Research

I am interested in the shapes and nature of dark matter halos surrounding our own and external galaxies. I study these through a combination of observations and N-body simulations of tidal streams and dwarf galaxy interactions.
I have previously done work on the morphology, explosive origin and and ionization state of supernova remnants.

Stream-Fanning of Stellar Streams

I modeled the dynamical evoltuion of the Milky Way globular cluster, Palomar 5, and used the morphology of its stellar tidal stream to rule out a triaxial shape of the dark matter halo in the inner parts of our own galaxy. The Palomar 5 stream formed large, two-dimensional "fans" when evolved in a triaxial halo, inconsistant with observations of the stream.

Gaseous Dwarf-Dwarf Interactions

I studied high resolution, resolved HI maps of a sample of 10 interacting dwarf galaxies at various interacting stages and in various environments within the Local Universe, and found that mutual interactions between the pairs "loosen up" their gas, but that ram-pressure stripping from a nearby massive galaxy is what ultimately removes the gas from the systems.

Supernova Remnants

Using observations from the NASA Chandra X-ray Observatory, we found that the supernova remnant, W49B, originites from a jet-driven, core-collapse explosion of a massive star and that its plasma is overionized (the ions are stripped of more electrons than they should be a the given temperature) due to a rapid adiabatic expansion.

I am a part of the TiNy Titans collaborations and the Stream-Team.
