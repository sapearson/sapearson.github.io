Tidal stream morphology as a potential probe

It has been predicted by simulations of the large scale structures in our Universe that galaxies should lie within triaxial dark matter halos. However, as we see galaxies in projection on the sky, it is difficult to assess the 3-dimensional shape of them.


Tidal streams of stars in the Milky Way offer a unique perspective on this problem. They form when stars leak out of star clusters, while they orbit as satellites around the Milky Way.


My work involves modeling the morphology and evolution of tidal streams in order to constrain the shape of the Milky Way’s dark matter halo.



Dwarf galaxy pairs in the local Universe

Investigating mergers between galaxies are crucial for our understanding of galaxy evolution throughout the history of the Universe. Although dwarf galaxies are the most numerous type of galaxy in the Universe, very little is known about their mutual interaction.


This project focuses on a sample of dwarf galaxy pairs in the local Universe, and the goal is that by studying the properties of these pairs, located either close to or far from a massive galaxy, our understanding of dwarf galaxy mergers through cosmic time will increase substantially.

X-ray analyses of supernova remnants

Supernova remnants are the result of the interaction between ejected material from a supernova explosion and the ambient medium surrounding the progenitor. I I have been involved in four different projects concerning supernova remnants:   


1) The morphology of supernova remnants in The Small Magellanic Cloud

2) The explosive origin of the Galactic supernova remnant, W49B

3) The overionization features in W49B
